Robot Design Studio: Powerhouse Finger

Cole Abbott, Heinrich Asbury, Jared Berry, Evan Bulatek, Benji Sobeloff-Gittes

A tendon-driven robotic finger optimized for strength and durability while preserving speed, range of motion, and functional dexterity.

Project Summary

Our capstone team designed and validated a 3-DOF robotic finger focused on high force output without sacrificing practical performance. The final system combines antagonistic tendon routing, coupled finger kinematics, custom pulleys and encoders, and a modular testbed to support robust bench testing and iterative prototyping.

In software and firmware, we built a full stack spanning simulation, controls, teleoperation, and safety-limited embedded motor control. The resulting platform demonstrates repeatable high-load behavior and provides a strong base for future sensing and manipulation tasks.

Hardware Description

Finger mounted on custom modular testbed.

Side profile view showing tendon-driven architecture.

Design iterations used to improve force and durability.

The hardware stack centers on robust tendon routing and modular construction so we can iterate quickly while maintaining high force output and repeatable performance.

  • 3-DOF finger with splay and flexion/extension.
  • PIP and DIP joints are mechanically coupled with an approximately linear relationship.
  • N+1 actuation: two differential tendons for antagonistic splay and strong flexion, one tendon for MCP flexion and PIP/DIP extension, one tendon for MCP extension.
  • Double-groove pulleys at each joint prevent tendon slacking without idler pulleys.
  • Parallel tensioning springs reduce slack and simplify assembly.
  • Knot tendon terminations on shafts for compact routing.
  • Z-stack construction supports faster prototyping, assembly, and part swaps.
  • Custom modular testbed includes finger and motor mounts plus pulley stand.
  • Custom off-axis joint encoders provide absolute position sensing.
  • Many components were manufactured in-house: pulleys, shafts, finger frames, and mounts.
  • Modular fingertip includes compliant contact surface and future tactile sensor mounting support.

Software Description

Firmware and Control Systems

The firmware and control architecture prioritizes safe torque delivery, reliable sensing, and flexible control interfaces for both bench testing and teleoperation.

  • Full firmware stack for encoder readout and motor driving.
  • Torque and current safety limits with soft-start behavior for safer startup.
  • Closed-loop joint feedback controller plus specialized value printing for plotting.
  • Torque-control mode teleoperation interface.
  • Rigorous manual validation performed across all controller types.

Simulation

The simulation toolchain mirrors the physical system, enabling validation of tendon dynamics, inverse kinematics, and controller performance before hardware deployment.

Demo 1: Catapult-style high-force release.

Demo 2: Weighted load interaction.

Demo 3: Fully simulated tendon tension behavior and force-sharing.

Demo 4: Fingertip trajectory tracking with IK.

  • Onshape assembly parsing into URDF using Blender, for collision and visualization in Drake and rviz2.
  • C++ and Eigen control library fingerlib with tendon tension solver, IK, and geometry constants.
  • Drake simulation integrated with ROS2 and simulated motors plus tendons.
  • Three control modes: joint torque, joint position, and fingertip position.
  • ROS2 wrapper nodes for fingerlib functions and visualization.
  • Workspace and Jacobian generation scripts for broad joint-state sweeps.
  • Trajectory tracking tools and plotting for torques, tensions, joint states, and fingertip states.
  • Full fingerlib test suite and robust initialization error handling.
